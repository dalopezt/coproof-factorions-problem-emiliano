**Theorem (Upper Bound).** For all $n \in \mathbb{N}$, if $n > 2540160$, then the digit factorial sum of $n$ is strictly less than $n$; that is,
$$\forall\, n \in \mathbb{N},\quad n > 2540160 \implies \sigma(n) < n,$$
where $\sigma(n) = \sum_{d \in \text{digits}(n)} d!$ denotes the sum of the factorials of the decimal digits of $n$.
